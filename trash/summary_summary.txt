SUPREME COURT JUDGMENT SUMMARY
======================================================================

Case: Civil Appeal No. 8539 of 2024
Court: Supreme Court of India
Date: January 12, 2026
Bench: Dipankar Datta, Augustine George Masih

FACTUAL BACKGROUND
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Appellant is an association of flat buyers which entered into Flat Buyer Agreements with ACIPL for units in Ansal Crown Heights impugned order NCDRC ACIPL individual builder buyer agreements, ACIPL promised to handover possession of the apartments within a period of months from the date of execution of the agreements, which expired for all the buyers in the time period from December, 2013 - December, 2015.

LEGAL ISSUE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The issue is whether persons who were arrayed as respondents in the consumer complaints but ultimately against whom no notice was issued and the complaints did not proceed, can be brought within the net of execution, on the premise that they were directors/promoters of the judgment-debtor company.

KEY HOLDINGS & LEGAL REASONING
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Once the lis stood consciously and finally confined to ACIPL, the adjudication culminated in an order binding exclusively ACIPL and none else.

2. The order neither records any determination of liability against the respondents to nor contains any direction requiring them to perform or refrain from performing any act.

3. It is trite that a decree cannot, by process of execution, be employed to shift or enlarge liability so as to bind persons who were neither parties to the decree nor otherwise legally liable thereunder.

4. The CP Act envisages a complete adjudicatory process founded on service of notice, pleadings, opportunity to contest, leading of evidence, and recorded findings of fact and law.

FINAL ORDER
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Consequently, the appeals are dismissed. However, this dismissal will not preclude the appellant from pursuing any remedy available in law against the promoters/directors, including proceedings under the Companies Act, IBC, or civil law, should the statutory requirements therefor be satisfied.
