The SEBI's complaint alleges that the Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission issued an advertisement on 5th March 2021 notifying the examination for recruitment of Samiksha Adhikari/Sahayak Samiksh Akhikari. In the advertisement, the applicant, unaware of the verification proceedings, filed an affidavit clarifying the position and declaring the pendency of two cases against him. The SEBI alleges that the District Magistrate had found no legal impediment in his appointment; he had not been charge-sheeted in the offences alleged against him; mere nonclosure is not always fatal; he has been acquitted in the other case; and he had himself, albeit subsequently, disclosed the cases against him to the SEBI. The SEBI also alleges that the respondent, unaware of these verification proceedings, signed the affidavit and filed an affidavit, which was subsequently signed by the district magistrate. The SEBI seeks a permanent injunction, an officer-and-director bar, and a conduct-based injunction. The litigation will be led by Sanjay Wadhwa and supervised by C. J. Rawat.
